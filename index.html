POWERLINES

Just like any other wiring or electrical equipment, powerlines produce electric and magnetic fields. These fields are also known as extremely low frequency fields, or ELF fields (Ministry of Health NZ). Powerlines transfer electricity to power tools and machines ued to connect to various networks. For this reason Powerlines are a crutial and constant part of our lives. Alongside this powerliines allow us to connect to phone lines, which connect to telephone landlines in the world while also providing connection to the internet. It is an excellent way of connecting everyone together.

ANTENNAS

Antennas are the metal rod or dish that catches radio waves and turns them into electrical signals feeding into something like a radio or television or a telephone system. Antennas like this are sometimes called receivers (mobilesystems.co.nz). In the 21st century most of our internet is provided through cables and powerlines however, if you look around the city antennas still exist and they are everywhere! The antennas allow multiple wireless networking for many purposes. The photos of antennas below provide access to netowrk TV as they are residential amtennas. Over time people are using antennas less as TV is now becoming interconnected with social media and the internet.

MANHOLE COVERS

Manhole covers are the entry ways to a system deep down below us. These systems include thousands and thoushands of cables that allow us to send data from our homes to the outside world. The are located on the ground on mostly roads (sometimes the footpath) distanced verey few 100m away form eachother.

SECURITY CAMERAS

Security cameras, also called CCTV which stands for closed-circuit telecision, has become a necessity in out lives. These cameras are used to transmit a signal to a specific place, on a limited set of monitors. CCTVs are mainly used to keep palces safe by preventing crime. In the generaton we live in now it had become a norm to have CCTVs. It is almose impossible to find a public place that is left unmoniotred by these cameras. When these cameras record footage, they are constantly sending the recored information to wider networks.
